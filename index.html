About Blind Cricket

Blind cricket is similar to sighted cricket, but there are certain modifications made to accommodate visually challenged players.



A Blind Cricket Ball:

Differs significantly from a traditional leather cricket ball. Instead, it is a uniquely designed hard plastic sphere equipped with precision bearings.For visually challenged players, this ball becomes their guide on the field, as they rely on the distinct sound produced by the embedded bearings to locate and engage with it.


Stumps are made with iron and have bearings attached at the top.

Blind Cricket Categories

Blind cricket is divided into three categories:
B1, B2, & B3.


B1 Players:

B1 players are completely blind and have no vision. Due to their lack of sight, certain advantages are given to them. For example, they are allowed to take one bounce catch. If a B1 player takes a single run, it is considered as a double because batting is more challenging for B1 players. B1 players are required to wear blindfold goggles for fairness. Additionally, B1 players have to bowl 40% of the overs in a match.



B2 Players:

B2 players are partially blind and can see at a distance of two to four feet.



B3 Players:

B3 players are partially sighted with a vision range of six to eight feet.

Playing 11

To form a playing 11 in blind cricket, it is mandatory to include
4 B1 players, 3 B2 players, and 4 B3 players.

Blind Cricket Formats:

Blind cricket is usually played in two formats worldwide: T20 and OD (One Day). However, in blind cricket, the OD format differs from sighted cricket. In blind cricket, OD matches consist of 40 overs, whereas in sighted cricket, it is a fifty overs format.



ICC WBCC (World Blind Cricket Council):

Just like the ICC WBCC (World Blind Cricket Council) serves as the global governing body for blind cricket.

Our Goal

We strive to offer opportunities to exceptionally talented visually impaired cricketers from Vidarbha, allowing them to showcase their skills and potential.Our aspiration is to proudly represent Vidarbha on the national stage.blind cricket in the rural regions of Vidarbha and identifying new talent remain among our top priorities.
